Learning HTML/CSS

Learning the basics of HTML/CSS programming is fundamental to our program. Participants will learn the essentials of designing and developing a website. The curriculum culminates in participants creating their own webpage from scratch. Participants will learn how to incorporate images, links, and buttons into their page.

Using Word, Excel, Powerpoint

In order to familiarize participants to the working environment, we will begin class with a brief introduction to the Microsoft Office Suite. Participants will be able to navigate Word, Powerpoint, and Excel. Word and Powerpoint are powerful tools to ideate potential designs for a webpage layout.

Promoting Collaboration

Throughout our undergraduate studies, all three members of Two X Ventures have struggled with computer science courses. We understand the difficulty of grappling with concepts in addition to the challenge of asking for assistance. We hope to provide our participants with a gradual but informative introduction into a technical and entrepreneurial mindset.

Venture Creation

Our curriculum is centered entirely around building the independence and confidence necessary to ideate and launch a venture. Throughout the fifteen-day program, participants will have the opportunity to learn skills and build familiarity with valuable tools, such as the Microsoft Office Suite and HTML/CSS programming, while actively applying that knowledge to entrepreneurial ideas. In the final three days of the program, participants will have the opportunity to participate in a Hackathon and collaborate in teams to ultimately pitch their own start-up.

A LITTLE ABOUT US

Who are we?

Two X Ventures is an all-female team that is passionate about technology. Our members are studying Mathematics, Statistics, and Computer Science at Columbia University. We strongly believe in mentorship and have previous experience as tutors, teachers, and teaching assistants for our local communities and abroad. We're excited to harness our diverse backgrounds to collaborate as a team and continue empowering others through STEM education.

OUR LOCATION

WHY TEL AVIV

The community our project hopes to work with is the LGBTQA Center in Tel Aviv, Israel. The LGBTQA Center welcomes individuals from the surrounding community and facilitates the LGBTQ Refugee Program. The project provides a variety of assistance, including legal assistance, financial support, and fundamental tools to access employment, to Palestinians, Africans, and Israelis from minority groups who identify as LGBTQA and would otherwise be persecuted in their own communities. We want to equip these refugees with marketable skills so that they may find employment easier.

  • THE PLAN

Two X Ventures addresses systemic economic concerns of refugees by providing the mentorship necessary to ultimately increase employability and inclusivity in Tel Aviv’s technology industry. We hope to equip LGBTQA refugees with an entrepreneurial mindset, computer familiarity, and intermediate knowledge of web development languages HTML/CSS in order to assist refugees in developing confidence, independence, and marketable skills.

Our project seeks to provide LGBTQ refugees from Arab countries in Tel Aviv with a computer education and more broadly expose these individuals to STEM fields. We hope to familiarize refugees with using computers in addition to equipping them with lifelong skills in essential website development. Participants will grow towards the independence necessary to create their own webpage. We seek to supplement these courses with an entrepreneurial focus and teach participants how to develop small ventures and use these webpages as a means to communicate the focus and intent of any business that they create in the future. By the end of our sixteen day program, participants will be equipped with HTML/CSS skills, a strategic technical mindset, and knowledge of the process of small venture creation.

Over the first twelve days, we will assist participants in developing their own web page and practice pitching in small groups. Each participant will be encouraged to imagine their own small venture then create a webpage based on that idea. The initial two deliverables, the webpage and the pitch, will be developed individually so that each participant receives hands-on experience and sufficient mentoring. We will have participants pitch their small venture and webpages in small groups. In the last three days of the program, we will facilitate an intensive hackathon. Participants will be given a theme/problem and work in groups of four to develop both a webpage and small venture pitch then present both elements to the entire class. By the end of our program, we hope that the participants will emerge with marketable skills that give them a competitive edge in the labor market in addition to the tools necessary to ideate and launch a small venture.
